# Payattu — System Explanation

> A traditional community-based rotating contribution system practised in Kerala, India.

---

## What is Payattu?

**Payattu** (also known as *Nattupayattu*) is a rotating informal credit system common among community groups, families, and friends. It is entirely trust-based — no banks, no paperwork, no interest — just mutual obligation recorded and honoured over years.

---

## How It Works — Step by Step

### 1. A Member Hosts an Event

A community member (the **Host**) organises a **Payattu event** at a date, time, and place. They invite other community members.

> Example: **Ali** hosts a Payattu at Mishkath Hall on 15 December 2025, 4PM.

---

### 2. You Attend and Contribute

When you attend the event, you give a **sum of money** to the host as your contribution.

> Example: You give **₹1,000** to Ali.

This contribution is recorded as **pending** — meaning Ali owes you a return in the future.

```
You → Ali → ₹1,000 → PENDING
```

---

### 3. The Cycle Continues Over Time

Over months or years, you attend multiple events hosted by different people, contributing to each.

```
You → Ali   → ₹1,000 → PENDING
You → Biju  → ₹1,500 → PENDING
You → Faris → ₹800   → PENDING
```

These amounts accumulate. There is **no deadline**, no automatic reminders between parties — it's understood within the community.

---

### 4. You Decide to Host Your Own Event

After some time (typically 2–3 years), **you** host a Payattu event. You invite the people who owe you a return.

All the people you contributed to in the past are now obligated to come and **give back** to you.

---

### 5. They Return the Amount — with a Custom Bonus

When they attend your Payattu, each person gives back:

```
Return Amount = Original Amount + Custom Bonus
```

The **bonus** is a manually agreed amount (typically a cultural gesture of gratitude). There is **no automatic interest calculation** — the bonus is decided individually.

> Example:
> Ali comes to your Payattu and gives **₹1,200** (original ₹1,000 + ₹200 bonus).

At this point:
- The old record `You → Ali → ₹1,000 → PENDING` is marked as **SETTLED**
- A new record is created: `Ali → You → ₹1,200 → PENDING`

```
OLD:  You → Ali → ₹1,000 → SETTLED ✓
NEW:  Ali → You → ₹1,200 → PENDING
```

---

### 6. The Cycle Repeats

Now Ali has a new pending obligation with you. When Ali hosts again in the future, you will return their ₹1,200 + a bonus.

This **rotating cycle of mutual obligation** continues indefinitely within the community.

---

## Key Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| **No automatic interest** | Bonus is manually entered each time |
| **No partial payments** | Full amount returned at once |
| **No expiry** | Pending amounts remain until returned |
| **Historical records** | Every transaction is kept, never deleted |
| **Trust-based** | The system relies entirely on community honour |

**PayattuBook** is a personal digital ledger for tracking your Payattu obligations.

| Feature | What it does |

| **Add Event** | Record events you're invited to (host, date, place) |
| **Mark Payment** | After attending, record how much you gave |
| **History** | View all past contributions and returns |
| **Analytics** | See total amounts given and who owes you a return |
